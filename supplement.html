2. Mixed effects models

2.1. Perimetric complexity model

Model formula:

\[ \begin{aligned} \operatorname{Perimetric\_complexity}_{i} &\sim N \left(\alpha_{j[i]} + \beta_{1j[i]}(\operatorname{Relative\_frequency\_l}), \sigma^2 \right) \\ \left( \begin{array}{c} \begin{aligned} &\alpha_{j} \\ &\beta_{1j} \end{aligned} \end{array} \right) &\sim N \left( \left( \begin{array}{c} \begin{aligned} &\mu_{\alpha_{j}} \\ &\mu_{\beta_{1j}} \end{aligned} \end{array} \right) , \left( \begin{array}{cc} \sigma^2_{\alpha_{j}} & \rho_{\alpha_{j}\beta_{1j}} \\ \rho_{\beta_{1j}\alpha_{j}} & \sigma^2_{\beta_{1j}} \end{array} \right) \right) \text{, for ISO\_script j = 1,} \dots \text{,J} \end{aligned} \]

Model coefficients:

Characteristic Beta 95% CI1 p-value
Relative_frequency_l -2.4 -3.1, -1.7 <0.001
1 CI = Confidence Interval

The formula above with the coefficients plugged in:

\[ \begin{aligned} \operatorname{\widehat{Perimetric\_complexity}}_{i} &\sim N \left(40.41_{\alpha_{j[i]}} - 2.4_{\beta_{1j[i]}}(\operatorname{Relative\_frequency\_l}), \sigma^2 \right) \\ \left( \begin{array}{c} \begin{aligned} &\alpha_{j} \\ &\beta_{1j} \end{aligned} \end{array} \right) &\sim N \left( \left( \begin{array}{c} \begin{aligned} &0 \\ &0 \end{aligned} \end{array} \right) , \left( \begin{array}{cc} 11.3 & 0.14 \\ 0.14 & 1.32 \end{array} \right) \right) \text{, for ISO\_script j = 1,} \dots \text{,J} \end{aligned} \]

Model formula:

\[ \begin{aligned} \operatorname{Compression}_{i} &\sim N \left(\alpha_{j[i]} + \beta_{1j[i]}(\operatorname{Relative\_frequency\_l}), \sigma^2 \right) \\ \left( \begin{array}{c} \begin{aligned} &\alpha_{j} \\ &\beta_{1j} \end{aligned} \end{array} \right) &\sim N \left( \left( \begin{array}{c} \begin{aligned} &\mu_{\alpha_{j}} \\ &\mu_{\beta_{1j}} \end{aligned} \end{array} \right) , \left( \begin{array}{cc} \sigma^2_{\alpha_{j}} & \rho_{\alpha_{j}\beta_{1j}} \\ \rho_{\beta_{1j}\alpha_{j}} & \sigma^2_{\beta_{1j}} \end{array} \right) \right) \text{, for ISO\_script j = 1,} \dots \text{,J} \end{aligned} \]
